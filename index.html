Fun kayaking jargon no one understands

  1. Boof!
  2. Steeze
  3. Gnar

Boof

Boof is a verb meaning: to paddle at a location and intend to launch off of it, most often a rock with some water flowing over at least part of it. Used in a sentence: ' Bruh, follow me and boof this ledge.'

Steeze

The word steeze is most often used in a context as to refer to a move someone made as really impressive or cool. Used in a sentence 'Dude, that boof was so steeze!'

Gnar

the word Gnar refers to rapids that are really big, hard, impossible to kayak. Used in a sentence: 'that rapid is gnar!'
